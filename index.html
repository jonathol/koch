History

The Koch snowflake is a mathematical curve and one of the earliest fractal curves to have been described. It is based on the Koch curve, which appeared in a 1904 paper titled "On a continuous curve without tangents, constructible from elementary geometry" by the Swedish mathematician Helge von Koch.
The progression for the area of the snowflake converges to 1.6 times the area of the original triangle, while the progression for the snowflake's perimeter diverges to infinity. Consequently, the snowflake has a finite area bounded by an infinitely long line.
More details can be found here.

Construction

The Koch snowflake can be constructed by starting with an equilateral triangle, then recursively altering each line segment as follows:

  1. divide the line segment into three segments of equal length
  2. draw an equilateral triangle that has the middle segment from step 1 as its base and points outward
  3. remove the line segment that is the base of the triangle from step 2

Properties

Perimeter

The perimeter of the snowflake after n iterations is:
Pn = 3 * s * (4/3)n
where s is the length of the side.

Area

The area of the snowflake after n iterations is:
An = (a0/5) * (8-3(4/9)n)
where a0 is the area of the original triangle.
